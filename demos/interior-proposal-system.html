The starting point

Good work, scattered process.

A small interior studio usually has the design taste and the client trust. What slows it down is the back-and-forth — chasing brief details, rebuilding proposals from scratch, and losing track of who is at which stage.

What the day-to-day looks like

  • Leads from Instagram, referrals, WhatsApp, and walk-ins
  • Proposals made manually every time
  • Unclear project scope going into the first meeting
  • Repeated client questions about timeline and process
  • Inconsistent follow-up — some leads cool off
  • No lead pipeline — hard to know what is in flight

Asset 03

Sample proposal structure.

Same seven sections every time. New proposals are drafted from existing project material rather than rebuilt from a blank document.

Sample — Niva Spaces proposal

  1. 01

    Client brief

    A two-paragraph restatement of what the client wants, in their own words where possible.

  2. 02

    Scope

    Spaces in scope, level of intervention (cosmetic / refit / full redesign), and what is explicitly not included.

  3. 03

    Design approach

    Mood direction, materials palette, and a few reference projects from your portfolio.

  4. 04

    Phases

    Concept → drawings → execution → handover. Indicative timeline against each phase.

  5. 05

    Commercials

    Design fee, execution model, payment milestones, and what triggers each milestone.

  6. 06

    Exclusions

    Civil work, statutory approvals, or anything that depends on a third party — listed plainly.

  7. 07

    Next steps

    A clear yes / no path — site visit booking, advance, and the kickoff date if approved.

Asset 04

Sample follow-up sequence.

Four short messages over ten days. Each one assumes the lead is busy, and gives them an easy reply path.

Day 0

After enquiry

Acknowledge and send the requirement form

Thank you for reaching out. Here is a short form that helps us prepare for our first conversation. Once we have it, we suggest a couple of time slots for a call or studio visit.

Day 2

If form not filled

Gentle nudge with help

A quick reminder on the requirement form. Happy to fill this together over a 10-minute call if it is easier — share a slot that suits you.

Day 5

After consultation

Share a recap and indicative scope

Sharing a short recap of what we discussed and an indicative scope. The full proposal will follow once we agree this captures the brief correctly.

Day 10

If no response on proposal

Open a clear yes / no

Following up on the proposal sent last week. Happy to revise scope or commercials if either feels off — and equally happy to pause if the timing is not right yet.

Scope

Where AI helps.

AI helps with drafting, structuring, summarizing, and workflow support. It does not make design decisions, commit pricing, or replace the studio's judgment.

What it actually does

  • Turn WhatsApp notes and form responses into a structured client brief
  • Draft first proposal versions from approved templates
  • Create follow-up messages based on lead stage
  • Summarize site visit notes into scope points
  • Flag missing brief details before proposal creation
  • Prepare weekly pipeline summaries for the studio owner
  • Reuse past project descriptions and portfolio language consistently

Boundary

AI does not make design decisions, commit pricing, or replace client judgment. It helps structure information, draft assets, and reduce repetitive proposal work.
