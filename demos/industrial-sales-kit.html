The starting point

The messy reality before the sprint.

A typical industrial supplier already has the substance — capable machines, working drawings, repeat customers. What is missing is structure. Enquiries arrive on five channels, replies depend on who is at the desk, and product information lives in too many places to find quickly.

What the day-to-day looks like

  • Product details scattered across PDFs, Excel, WhatsApp, and old brochures
  • Outdated product page that does not reflect current capabilities
  • Slow RFQ replies — sometimes hours, sometimes days
  • No standard proposal format — every quote rebuilt from scratch
  • No enquiry tracker — open RFQs live in inboxes and notebooks
  • Follow-up depends on memory rather than a workflow

Sprint outputs

What a 5-day sprint could produce.

Five practical assets, drafted from your existing material and reviewed with you before delivery. Each one is something the sales and ops team can use the next morning.

01

Product / service landing page

A single, clean page that explains what you make, who you make it for, and how to send a serious enquiry.

02

Product catalog sample

A presentable PDF and web catalog drafted from existing drawings, spec sheets, and stock lists.

03

RFQ response template

A standard reply for email and WhatsApp that asks for the right inputs every time, in the same order.

04

Enquiry tracker

A simple sheet that captures every RFQ, its status, the quoted value, and the next follow-up date.

05

Owner dashboard

A weekly view of open enquiries, quote pipeline, and follow-ups due — designed to be read in two minutes.

Asset 03

Sample RFQ reply.

One template, used in email and WhatsApp, that captures the right inputs the first time. Saves a round-trip on most enquiries.

Sample — RFQ reply (email / WhatsApp)

Hello {{First name}},

Thank you for reaching out to Sahyadri Precision Components. We can help with this enquiry. To prepare an accurate quote, please share the following:

  1. 01 Drawing or reference image — PDF, STEP, or a clear photo with key dimensions
  2. 02 Material grade — e.g. EN8, EN24, SS304, Al 6061
  3. 03 Quantity — sample, batch size, or estimated annual volume
  4. 04 Tolerance class — on critical features, if not on the drawing
  5. 05 Delivery timeline — required by date
  6. 06 Delivery location — city and pin code

Once we have these, you will receive an indicative quote within one working day, and a firm quote after a quick review on our side.

Regards,
Rohan Kale
Sahyadri Precision Components

Scope

Where AI helps.

AI is used to speed up drafting, structuring, summarizing, and workflow support — not to automatically commit pricing or delivery promises.

What it actually does

  • Turn messy product notes into structured catalog entries
  • Draft RFQ replies from approved templates
  • Flag missing RFQ inputs before quotation
  • Summarize the enquiry pipeline for the owner
  • Create first drafts of product pages and proposal copy
  • Convert Excel and product lists into usable sales assets
  • Generate follow-up reminders and status summaries

Boundary

AI does not automatically commit pricing, tolerances, or delivery timelines. Those remain reviewed by the business owner or team.
